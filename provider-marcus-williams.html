About Marcus

Marcus Williams brings a unique perspective to mental health care, combining his lived experience as a Black man with professional training in culturally responsive therapy. He specializes in helping clients navigate anxiety, depression, and the unique stressors faced by Black communities.

His approach integrates evidence-based practices like Cognitive Behavioral Therapy with mindfulness techniques and cultural awareness, creating a therapeutic space that feels both professional and authentically connected to his clients' experiences.

Marcus believes that healing happens when clients feel truly seen and understood. He works to create an environment where Black men, in particular, can explore their emotions without judgment and develop healthy coping strategies.

Areas of Expertise

Anxiety Disorders

Generalized anxiety, social anxiety, and culturally-specific anxiety related to discrimination and microaggressions.

Depression

Major depression, seasonal depression, and depression related to systemic oppression and racial stress.

Men's Mental Health

Addressing unique challenges faced by Black men, including emotional expression and vulnerability.

Mindfulness & Coping

Stress management, mindfulness practices, and developing healthy coping mechanisms for daily life.

Therapeutic Philosophy

"

Mental health care should meet you where you are, not where others think you should be. I believe in the strength and resilience that already exists within my clients, and my role is to help them access and build upon that foundation.

Every person deserves therapy that honors their cultural background and lived experiences. Together, we create a space where healing can happen authentically.

- Marcus Williams, LPC
