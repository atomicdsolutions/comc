About The Foundation

Our story, mission, and commitment to culturally competent mental health advocacy

Our Origin Story

The Getting My Cheese Back On My Cracker Foundation was born from a simple but powerful need: two co-founders searching for community and struggling to find therapists who truly understood their cultural background and lived experiences.

What started as personal conversations about mental health challenges in Black and Brown communities evolved into a podcast, and that podcast became a movement dedicated to breaking down barriers and reducing stigma.

Today, we lead with vulnerability, sharing our mental health journeys so others can see themselves in our stories and know they're not alone in their struggles.

Our Mission

To improve mental health and wellness within Black and Brown communities by providing culturally competent resources, breaking down stigma, and creating safe spaces for healing and growth.

Our Vision

A world where every person in Black and Brown communities has access to culturally sensitive mental health support and feels empowered to prioritize their mental wellness without shame or judgment.

Our Core Values

Frequently Asked Questions

Common questions about our foundation and culturally competent mental health services

Culturally competent mental health care involves providers who understand and respect the cultural backgrounds, values, and experiences of their clients. This includes awareness of how factors like race, ethnicity, language, religion, and socioeconomic status impact mental health. Culturally competent therapists adapt their approaches to be more effective for diverse communities and address unique challenges like racial trauma and intergenerational stress.

Our provider directory is currently in development and will feature verified, culturally competent mental health professionals who understand the unique experiences of Black and Brown communities. You can join our newsletter to be notified when the directory launches, or reach out to us directly for current recommendations and resources.

We offer several community programs including Cooking for Calm (live cooking sessions with mental health conversations), Walktober & 5K events (movement for mental wellness), Remove the Stigma Forums (in-person conversations with mental health professionals), and three podcasts that create safe spaces for sharing and growth.

There are many ways to support our mission: volunteer at events, join our programs, participate in our communities, donate to support our work, or apply for board positions. You can also help by sharing our resources with others who might benefit and following us on social media to amplify our message.

While our foundation specifically focuses on addressing the unique mental health challenges faced by Black and Brown communities, we welcome allies and supporters from all backgrounds who share our commitment to mental health equity and cultural competence. Our resources and advocacy work benefit the broader movement for inclusive mental health care.

The name reflects the universal experience of feeling like something essential is missing from your life - like cheese sliding off a cracker. It represents the journey of mental wellness: finding balance, getting back to wholeness, and helping others do the same. It's about vulnerability, authenticity, and the community support needed to heal and thrive.
